## The Problem

### The Immune System: Medicine's Most Complex Frontier

The human immune system contains **billions of cells, thousands of proteins, and millions of possible states**—yet healthcare treats it with blunt instruments:

**1. Diagnostic Blind Spots**
- Current immune testing captures ~5% of relevant biomarkers
- Autoimmune diseases take **4.6 years on average** to diagnose
- Cancer immunotherapy response is unpredictable (only 20-40% of patients respond)
- COVID revealed how little we understand individual immune variation

**2. One-Size-Fits-All Treatment**
- $50B spent annually on immunotherapy with hit-or-miss results
- 23 million Americans with autoimmune diseases get standardized treatment
- Transplant rejection kills 10% of recipients despite immunosuppression
- Vaccines designed for "average" immune systems, leaving millions unprotected

**3. Preventive Immunity is Non-Existent**
- No systematic approach to strengthening immune function before disease
- Aging-related immune decline (immunosenescence) is accepted as inevitable
- Chronic inflammation—the silent killer—goes unmonitored in 95% of population

**4. Fragmented Data & Siloed Research**
- Immunology data scattered across labs, hospitals, and research institutions
- Each patient's immune history is essentially lost between providers
- $10B+ in redundant immunology research annually due to poor data sharing

### The Market Reality

- **$89B**: Annual spend on autoimmune disease treatment (growing 7% CAGR)
- **$150B**: Global immunotherapy market by 2030
- **$50B**: Vaccine market (personalized vaccines emerging)
- **$200B+**: Combined addressable market for immune intelligence

**The insight**: The immune system is the master regulator of health—from fighting cancer to aging to chronic disease. Yet we have no operating system to understand and optimize it.
