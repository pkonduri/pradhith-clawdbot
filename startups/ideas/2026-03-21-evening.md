## The Problem

### The Planning Paradox

Everyone knows they should plan. Almost no one does:

**The Avoidance Crisis**
- **67% of Americans** have no estate plan whatsoever
- **78% of Millennials** have no will (despite having children, assets, digital lives)
- Average person puts off planning for **7+ years** after first thinking about it
- **90% of estate plans** become outdated within 3 years and never get updated
- Talking about death remains taboo in most families

**The Complexity Barrier**
- Estate planning requires coordinating 15+ professionals (attorneys, financial advisors, CPAs, insurance agents)
- Average comprehensive estate plan costs **$3,000-$10,000+**
- DIY solutions are dangerously incomplete (LegalZoom wills miss 80% of considerations)
- State laws vary dramatically—what works in California fails in Texas
- Tax optimization requires expertise few possess

**The Digital Estate Disaster**
- Average person has **100+ online accounts** with $35,000+ in digital assets
- **70% of digital assets** are lost at death (crypto, photos, subscriptions, domains)
- No standard process for digital asset transfer
- Platforms have inconsistent death policies (some delete everything)
- Password managers don't solve the access-at-death problem

### The Execution Nightmare

Even when plans exist, execution fails:

**The Executor Burden**
- Executors spend **500+ hours** managing estates
- **60% of executors** have no idea what they're doing
- Critical documents scattered across locations
- No coordination between banks, attorneys, government, healthcare
- Grief-stricken families forced into administrative hell

**The Communication Breakdown**
- **73% of families** experience conflict during estate settlement
- Wishes aren't clearly communicated before death
- End-of-life preferences unknown until crisis moments
- Important conversations never happen
- Relationships destroyed by ambiguous intentions

**The Healthcare Disconnect**
- **80% of people** want to die at home; **60% die in hospitals**
- Advance directives not on file when needed
- DNR wishes unclear or inaccessible in emergencies
- Medical power of attorney complications
- Hospice engagement happens too late

### The Legacy Lost

The emotional dimension is completely unaddressed:

**The Memory Gap**
- Stories, wisdom, and context die with people
- Photo archives become inaccessible
- Family history undocumented
- Letters never written
- Video messages never recorded

**The Grief Vacuum**
- Families left with administrative chaos instead of closure
- No guidance for grief journey
- Memorial planning stressful and rushed
- Ongoing support non-existent
- Connection to deceased severed abruptly
