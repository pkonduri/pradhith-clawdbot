## The Problem: The Coming Climate Litigation Tsunami

### Climate Accountability Has Teeth Now

Climate change has moved from boardroom talking point to courtroom reality. The litigation wave that executives dismissed as "activism" is now delivering billion-dollar judgments:

**The Numbers Are Alarming:**
- **2,666 active climate cases** globally as of April 2026 (Sabin Center)
- **200%+ increase** in cases filed since 2020
- **$200B+ in damages** currently sought across pending cases
- **42 countries** with active climate litigation
- **70%+ of cases** now filed against corporations, not just governments
- **68 new cases filed** in Q1 2026 alone

**Landmark Cases Are Creating Precedent:**

| Case | Year | Outcome | Implication |
|------|------|---------|-------------|
| Milieudefensie v. Shell | 2021 | Shell ordered to cut emissions 45% by 2030 | Courts can mandate corporate climate action |
| Held v. Montana | 2023 | Youth plaintiffs win constitutional climate case | State climate obligations are enforceable |
| KlimaSeniorinnen v. Switzerland | 2024 | ECHR rules climate inaction violates human rights | International human rights angle opens new venues |
| ClientEarth v. Shell Directors | 2025 | Directors face personal liability for climate strategy | D&O insurance now exposed |
| Municipalities v. Big Oil | 2026 | First successful damages award: $4.2B | Floodgates officially open |

**The Science Has Caught Up:**

Attribution science — the ability to quantify how much a specific company's emissions contributed to a specific climate harm — has matured:

- **96%+ confidence** in human causation of climate change
- **Carbon Majors Database:** 100 companies responsible for 71% of emissions since 1988
- **Event Attribution:** Can now link specific extreme weather events to climate change
- **Proportional Attribution:** Calculating company-specific contribution to specific damages

This means plaintiffs can now prove causation with scientific precision.

### The Corporate Exposure Is Massive

**Every Major Company Is Vulnerable:**

The "safe harbors" that protected companies are eroding:

1. **Scope 3 Emissions:** Courts increasingly holding companies liable for entire value chain
2. **Historical Emissions:** Retroactive liability for decades of past emissions
3. **Greenwashing Claims:** False or misleading climate claims = fraud liability
4. **Failure to Adapt:** Not preparing for known climate risks = negligence
5. **Director Duties:** Personal liability for climate-incompetent governance
6. **Disclosure Gaps:** SEC, EU rules create new disclosure-based liability
