## 💡 The Problem

### The Hydrogen Paradox
Green hydrogen is the holy grail of clean energy — it can power ships, trucks, steel mills, and chemical plants without emissions. But today's production faces critical challenges:

1. **Freshwater dependency** — Traditional electrolysis requires purified freshwater, putting it in competition with human consumption
2. **Centralized mega-projects** — $10B+ facilities take 5-7 years to build and create single points of failure
3. **Equipment corrosion** — Seawater destroys electrolyzers in months, not years
4. **Grid constraints** — Large facilities strain electrical infrastructure
5. **Transportation costs** — Moving hydrogen from remote production sites costs $2-4/kg

### The Maritime Urgency
The International Maritime Organization mandates 20% alternative fuel adoption by 2030 and net-zero by 2050. Ports are scrambling. They need hydrogen *at the dock*, not shipped from inland.

### The European Energy Crisis
Post-2022, Europe learned the hard way about energy dependency. They're throwing hundreds of billions at energy independence. Hydrogen is central to their strategy, but large projects move too slowly.
