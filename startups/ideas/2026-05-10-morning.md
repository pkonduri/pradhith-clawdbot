## 🔥 The Burning Problem

**The AI Delegation Trust Crisis Has Arrived**

Research published this week confirms what enterprises are discovering the hard way: **LLMs corrupt your documents when you delegate**. Subtle modifications, hallucinated facts, instruction drift, and silent errors are plaguing every organization deploying AI agents.

The symptoms are everywhere:
- Legal teams discovering AI-drafted contracts with invented clauses
- Financial analysts finding AI reports with hallucinated numbers
- Developers shipping AI-generated code with security vulnerabilities
- Researchers citing AI-fabricated sources
- Meta employees creating "agents to find agents, agents to rate agents" — pure chaos

**The core issue:** When you delegate work to AI, you have no reliable way to verify the AI did what you asked without corrupting, hallucinating, or deviating from your intent.

Current solutions are failing:
- **Observability tools** (Datadog, AgentOps) tell you *that* something happened, not *whether it was correct*
- **Fact-checking tools** verify individual claims, not entire workflows
- **Human review** doesn't scale and misses subtle corruptions
- **AI reviewing AI** creates recursive trust problems

**The billion-dollar question:** How do you trust AI to do work on your behalf?

## 💡 The Solution: Witness

**Witness is the enterprise verification layer for AI work delegation.**

When any AI agent performs work on behalf of humans, Witness:

### 1. **Intent Capture** 
Before delegation begins, Witness captures:
- Original source documents and data
- Human instructions and specifications
- Success criteria and constraints
- Compliance requirements

### 2. **Real-Time Monitoring**
As the AI works, Witness observes:
- Every action, tool call, and decision
- Intermediate states and reasoning
- Resource access and modifications
- Deviation from expected behavior

### 3. **Output Verification**
When work completes, Witness validates:
- Adherence to original specifications
- Factual accuracy against source truth
- Document integrity (no corruption)
- Instruction compliance
- Citation and reference verification

### 4. **Trust Scoring & Certification**
Witness provides:
- Per-output confidence scores
- Anomaly detection and alerts
- Verification certificates for compliance
- Audit trails for governance

### 5. **Continuous Learning**
The system improves through:
- Human feedback on verification accuracy
- Pattern detection across organizations
- Industry-specific verification models
- Emerging threat identification

### Core Technologies

1. **Differential Analysis Engine**
   - Compares source documents to AI outputs
   - Detects unauthorized modifications
   - Identifies insertion/deletion patterns
   - Semantic drift detection

2. **Specification Language (WitnessSpec)**
   - Declarative language for defining expected outputs
   - Constraint specification for AI behavior
   - Automated spec extraction from natural language
   - Integration with existing AI prompts

3. **Multi-Modal Verification**
   - Text: Semantic comparison, fact extraction
   - Code: Static analysis, security scanning, logic verification
   - Data: Schema validation, statistical anomaly detection
   - Documents: Structure integrity, citation verification

4. **Cryptographic Audit Trail**
   - Immutable logging of all AI actions
   - Merkle tree-based verification
   - Compliance-ready export formats
   - Integration with enterprise audit systems
