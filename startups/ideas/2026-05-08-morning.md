## The $13 Trillion Problem

**The global construction industry is catastrophically broken.**

- **Global housing deficit:** 3 billion people need adequate housing by 2030

**The traditional construction model is fundamentally incompatible with 21st-century demands:**

| Craft-based, variable quality | Manufactured, consistent quality |

**The $13T global construction market is ripe for disruption.**

## Why Modular Is Winning (But Still Losing)

**Modular construction promises everything:**
- ✅ 50-70% faster construction
- ✅ 20-40% cost reduction
- ✅ 90% less on-site waste
- ✅ 80% fewer weather delays
- ✅ Consistent factory quality
- ✅ Solves labor shortage

**Yet modular remains only 5% of US construction. Why?**

### The Modular Execution Gap

**1. Design Chaos**
- Architects design buildings that can't be modularized
- No feedback loop between design decisions and manufacturing constraints
- Every project reinvents the wheel
- Design changes cascade through production, destroying economics

**2. Factory Complexity**
- Each factory operates as an island
- No standardization across manufacturers
- Scheduling is done manually with spreadsheets
- Equipment utilization averages only 55%

**3. Logistics Nightmare**
- Modules are massive (often 60' x 15' x 12')
- Route planning requires permits, escort vehicles, bridge clearances
- Delivery timing must synchronize perfectly with crane availability
- One late module delays entire project

**4. Site Assembly Risk**
- Crane operations are dangerous and expensive
- Module alignment requires skilled labor
- MEP (mechanical/electrical/plumbing) connections are manual
- Weather windows are critical

**5. Capital Inefficiency**
- Factories require $50-200M investment
- Utilization is unpredictable
- Working capital trapped in finished inventory
- No marketplace for excess capacity

**The result:** Modular projects frequently fail to deliver promised savings, damaging the industry's reputation.

**The core problem isn't modular construction — it's the lack of intelligence infrastructure.**
