## The Opportunity

### The Trigger
Today's news: The UK just passed new laws making it mandatory for companies to offer easy subscription cancellation and refunds. The US FTC's "click-to-cancel" rule is now in effect. The EU's Digital Services Act is cracking down on dark patterns. Australia, Canada, and others are following.

**The subscription economy is $300B+ and growing — but it's built on friction.**

Every major subscription company has optimized for making cancellation hard:
- Hidden cancellation flows
- Retention dark patterns
- Confusing billing cycles
- "Pause" instead of cancel
- Phone-only cancellation

**The regulatory hammer is falling. Companies need to comply. Consumers are fed up.**

### The Problem

**For Businesses:**
- New regulations require "click-to-cancel" — one step, same channel as signup
- Non-compliance means massive fines (FTC: up to $50K per violation)
- Legacy billing systems weren't built for easy cancellation
- No standardized way to implement compliant flows
- Fear of revenue loss is delaying compliance

**For Consumers:**
- Average American has 12+ active subscriptions
- 42% forget about subscriptions they're still paying for
- Cancellation processes are intentionally frustrating
- No unified view of recurring payments
- Getting refunds for unwanted charges is nearly impossible

### The Insight

This is a **dual-sided market opportunity**:

1. **B2B Compliance Platform:** Help subscription businesses implement legally compliant, user-friendly cancellation while preserving revenue through legitimate retention

2. **B2C Consumer Platform:** Give consumers an AI agent that tracks, manages, audits, and cancels subscriptions on their behalf

**The two sides create a powerful network effect:** Businesses that implement SubscriptionOS get listed in our consumer app as "compliant" — driving trust and signups. Consumers using our app generate demand for businesses to adopt our platform.
