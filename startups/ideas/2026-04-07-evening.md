## Technology Deep Dive

### Voice Identity Encoding

Traditional voice biometrics use speaker verification models that require stored audio. Our approach:

1. **Enrollment:** User provides 30+ seconds of varied speech
2. **Encoding:** Neural encoder produces 512-dimensional voiceprint
3. **Hashing:** Voiceprint hashed with cryptographic commitment scheme
4. **Registration:** Hash anchored to distributed ledger with timestamp
5. **Verification:** Any audio can be checked against voiceprint without revealing the original

**Privacy Properties:**
- Raw audio never stored
- Voiceprint cannot reconstruct speech
- Verification without identity disclosure possible

### Clone Detection Architecture

```
Audio Input → Preprocessing → Feature Extraction → Similarity Search → Match Scoring

                                    ↓
                           Watermark Detection
                                    ↓
                           Synthesis Artifacts
                                    ↓
                           Confidence Ensemble
                                    ↓
                              Alert/Report
```

**Detection Modalities:**
- **Voiceprint similarity:** Compare against registered identities
- **Watermark presence:** Check for legitimate licensing markers
- **Synthesis artifacts:** Detect telltale signs of AI generation
- **Acoustic anomalies:** Identify impossible vocal characteristics

**Performance Targets:**
- 99.5% true positive rate on known clones
- <0.1% false positive rate
- <100ms latency for real-time detection
- Scale to 1M+ queries per day

### Watermarking System

Our invisible audio watermarks:
- Survive MP3 compression down to 64kbps
- Persist through format conversion
- Withstand speed changes up to 20%
- Remain detectable after noise addition
- Embed license ID, timestamp, and terms
