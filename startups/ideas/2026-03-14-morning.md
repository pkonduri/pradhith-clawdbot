# GrantOS — Autonomous Grant Writing & Research Funding Intelligence

## The $2 Trillion Opportunity Nobody's Automated

**Every year, $2+ trillion flows into research funding globally.** NIH alone distributes $50B annually. The EU's Horizon Europe: $100B. Private foundations: hundreds of billions more.

Yet the process of capturing this funding remains stuck in 1995: researchers manually searching databases, spending 200+ hours per grant application, achieving success rates of just 10-20%, and losing countless hours that could be spent on actual research.

**GrantOS is the autonomous intelligence layer that transforms how research funding flows from opportunity to award.**

## The Problem: The Grant Writing Tax

### The Hidden Cost of Research Funding

**For Researchers:**
- Average PI spends **40% of time** on grant writing instead of research
- 200+ hours per major NIH R01 application
- Success rates: NIH ~20%, NSF ~25%, most foundations <15%
- Rejection means starting over with minimal feedback
- Junior researchers lack institutional knowledge of what wins

**For Research Institutions:**
- $50B+ spent annually on grant administration in the US alone
- Fragmented systems: pre-award, compliance, post-award disconnected
- High administrative burden leads to researcher burnout
- Lost opportunities from lack of funding intelligence
- Compliance violations risk millions in clawbacks

**For Funders:**
- Review backlogs delay scientific progress
- Inconsistent evaluation despite rubrics
- Struggle to find the right researchers for priority areas
- Limited ability to track real-world impact of funded research

## The Solution: GrantOS

### Autonomous Intelligence Across the Entire Funding Lifecycle

**1. Opportunity Intelligence Engine**
- Real-time monitoring of 50,000+ funding sources globally
- AI matching: researcher profiles → funding opportunities
- Predictive scoring: likelihood of success per opportunity
- Strategic timing: when to apply based on funder patterns
- Gap analysis: what's being funded vs. researcher capabilities

**2. Autonomous Grant Composer**
- AI drafts complete grant applications from research summaries
- Learns winning patterns from 10M+ successful grants
- Adapts to specific funder requirements automatically
- Generates all required sections: specific aims, methodology, budget, broader impacts
- Maintains researcher voice while optimizing for success

**3. Collaboration Orchestrator**
- Identifies optimal co-investigators based on expertise gaps
- Builds multi-institutional teams automatically
- Manages letters of support workflow
- Coordinates preliminary data compilation
- Facilitates mentorship pairing for junior researchers

**4. Review Simulation Engine**
- AI simulates peer review before submission
- Identifies weaknesses reviewers will flag
- Provides specific improvement recommendations
- Benchmarks against successful proposals in same mechanism
- Predicts review scores with 90%+ accuracy

**5. Compliance Autopilot**
- Real-time budget validation against funder rules
- Automatic conflict of interest screening
- IRB/IACUC protocol alignment checking
- Export control and security classification analysis
- Continuous monitoring throughout project lifecycle

**6. Post-Award Intelligence**
- Automated progress report generation
- Spending optimization to maximize impact
- Renewal timing and strategy recommendations
- Publication-to-impact tracking
- Derivative funding opportunity identification

## Why Now: The Perfect Storm

### 1. AI Capabilities Have Crossed the Threshold
Large language models now write at publication quality. Combined with domain-specific training on millions of successful grants, AI can generate compelling, technically accurate proposals that match researcher intent.

### 2. Research Funding is Exploding
- CHIPS Act: $52B for semiconductor R&D
- IRA: $370B for climate/clean energy research
- ARPA-H: New $2.5B health research agency
- EU Horizon Europe: $100B through 2027
- Private foundation giving up 15% YoY

### 3. Researcher Burnout is Critical
Post-pandemic, 40% of researchers report considering leaving academia. Grant writing burden is the #1 cited reason. Institutions must reduce administrative load or lose talent.

### 4. Funders Want Better Outcomes
NIH, NSF, and major foundations are actively seeking ways to reduce bias, increase diversity, and fund more innovative research. AI-assisted review and matching aligns with these goals.

### 5. Competitive Pressure
International competition for research leadership is intensifying. China's R&D spending growth outpaces US. Efficiency in funding capture is now a national priority.
