# DataWorker — The Labor Union for the AI Age

## Executive Summary

**One-liner:** The collective bargaining platform that turns employee data into worker equity in the AI economy.

**The Problem:** Companies like Meta are capturing employee keystrokes, mouse movements, and screen content to train AI — then laying off 10% of their workforce. Workers generate the data that makes AI valuable, but have zero ownership, zero compensation, and zero leverage.

**The Solution:** DataWorker is a platform that enables workers to form "Data Unions" — collective bargaining units that negotiate data rights, compensation, and governance over how their work trains AI. Think AFL-CIO meets web3 for the AI age.

**Why Now:** 
- Meta just announced keystroke/mouse tracking for AI training (April 21, 2026)
- Mass layoffs despite record AI investment (Meta 10%, Amazon 30K, Block 50%)
- European GDPR creates regulatory arbitrage opportunities
- Worker sentiment is at a breaking point
- $100B+ annual spend on AI training data with zero going to workers

**Market Size:** $47B global labor union market + $120B AI training data market = massive TAM

**Business Model:** SaaS platform fees + transaction fees on data licensing deals + enterprise compliance tools

## The Opportunity

### The Meta Moment

On April 21, 2026, Reuters broke the story: Meta is installing "Model Capability Initiative" (MCI) software on employee computers to capture:
- Mouse movements
- Clicks and keystrokes
- Screen snapshots
- App and website interactions

The stated purpose? Training AI models to replicate how humans interact with computers. The subtext? Your work is now training data, and you get nothing.

Meanwhile, Meta announced 10% global layoffs starting May 20. The message is clear: **workers create the data, AI gets the credit, workers get the pink slip.**

### The Data Extraction Economy

Every major tech company is now an AI training data extraction operation:
- Amazon trimmed 30K corporate employees while ramping AI
- Block cut 50% of staff "to focus on AI"
- Microsoft, Google, Apple all mandating AI tool usage

Workers are generating trillions of data points daily:
- Code commits and review patterns
- Document editing behaviors
- Communication patterns
- Decision-making workflows
- Creative processes

This data is worth billions. The workers who generate it? Worthless, apparently.

### Why Workers Have No Power

Individual workers face an impossible situation:
1. **No visibility:** They don't know what data is being collected
2. **No portability:** They can't take their data contribution history with them
3. **No leverage:** One person refusing monitoring gets fired
4. **No compensation:** Data value accrues 100% to employer
5. **No legal protection:** US federal law has zero limits on workplace surveillance

The only solution is **collective action**.

## The Solution: DataWorker

### Core Product: Data Union Platform

DataWorker enables workers to form, join, and operate Data Unions — collective bargaining units focused on data rights in the AI economy.

#### For Workers:

**1. Union Formation & Membership**
- Create industry-specific or company-specific Data Unions
- Verified membership via employment credentials
- Anonymous participation options for at-risk workers
- Cross-employer solidarity coalitions

**2. Data Rights Dashboard**
- Real-time monitoring of what data employers collect
- Tracking software that logs logging software
- Data contribution quantification (how much AI training value you've generated)
- Privacy violation alerts

**3. Collective Bargaining Tools**
- Template data rights agreements
- Negotiation playbooks
- Strike coordination (data strikes = refusing AI tool usage)
- Legal resources and regulatory filing assistance

**4. Compensation Mechanisms**
- Data contribution tracking across employers
- Royalty distribution when models trained on member data are deployed
- Severance enhancement based on data contribution value
- "Data retirement accounts" that vest over time

#### For Employers (Yes, B2B Too):

**5. Compliance Platform**
- GDPR/state privacy law compliance automation
- Transparent data collection policies
- Audit trails for AI training data provenance
- Reduce legal risk from worker data disputes

**6. Fair Data Licensing**
- License worker data ethically with full consent
- Premium "clean" training data with documented provenance
- Avoid the reputational risk of the Meta approach
- ESG-friendly AI development

## Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: Legal/Regulatory Backlash

**Threat:** Employers challenge union legitimacy, anti-organizing tactics
**Mitigation:** 
- Legal defense fund from Day 1
- Partner with established labor law firms
- Structure as nonprofit foundation + for-profit operating company
- International incorporation for regulatory arbitrage

### Risk 2: Tech Company Retaliation

**Threat:** Meta/Google/Amazon actively suppress organizing
**Mitigation:**
- Anonymous membership options
- Distributed infrastructure (hard to shut down)
- Regulatory allies (FTC, European Commission)
- Public pressure campaigns ready to deploy

### Risk 3: Worker Apathy

**Threat:** Workers don't care enough to organize
**Mitigation:**
- Start with most motivated (just-laid-off workers)
- Immediate value (monitoring tools) before asking for commitment
- Gamification and community features
- Concrete wins early (even small victories)

### Risk 4: Business Model Viability

**Threat:** Hard to monetize worker movement
**Mitigation:**
- B2B revenue provides stability
- Data licensing creates scalable transaction revenue
- Legal services have proven economics
- Freemium B2C keeps acquisition costs low
