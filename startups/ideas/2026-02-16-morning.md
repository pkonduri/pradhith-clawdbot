## The Problem

### The Dirty Secret of Fashion & Retail

Every year, the fashion industry produces **150 billion garments**. **30% never get sold.** Until now, the "solution" was destruction:

- **Burberry** famously burned $37M of goods in a single year
- **H&M** incinerated 12 tons of unsold clothing annually
- **Amazon** destroyed millions of items per year
- **Nike** slashed unsold sneakers to prevent resale

This created a perverse incentive: protecting brand image by destroying perfectly good products.

### The Regulatory Hammer is Falling

**EU Regulation (February 2026):**
- Complete ban on destruction of unsold textiles, clothing, footwear, and accessories
- Mandatory transparency reporting on unsold inventory fate
- Heavy fines for non-compliance (up to 4% of global revenue)
- Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) fees based on circularity metrics

**Similar regulations enacted or pending:**
- France (Loi AGEC - already active)
- Germany, Netherlands, Belgium (in force)
- California SB-707 (pending)
- UK Extended Producer Responsibility (2027)
- China Circular Economy Promotion Law (expanding)

This isn't a trend â€” it's a global regulatory reality that every brand must address **now**.

### Why Current Solutions Fail

**Manual Liquidation:**
- Slow, relationship-dependent
- Value recovery: 10-30 cents on dollar
- No optimization across channels
- Compliance documentation nightmare

**Outlet Channels:**
- Limited capacity
- Brand dilution concerns
- Geographic constraints
- Not a solution for all SKUs

**Donation:**
- Complex logistics
- Limited tax benefits
- "Dumping" concerns in receiving markets
- Still requires compliance documentation

**Material Recycling:**
- Less than 1% of clothing gets recycled into new clothing
- Technology limitations
- Economics don't work at scale

**The Real Problem:** Brands have no **intelligent system** to route the right product to the right channel at the right time while maintaining compliance across jurisdictions.
