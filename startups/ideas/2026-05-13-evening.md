## Technology Deep Dive

### The OlfactoryNet Foundation Model

**Architecture:**
- Molecular encoder (graph neural network for chemical structures)
- Perceptual decoder (transformer predicting human smell experience)
- Cross-modal bridges (smell ↔ language ↔ vision)

**Training Data:**
- 50M+ molecular structures with smell annotations
- 10M+ consumer preference data points (licensed)
- 1M+ expert perfumer evaluations
- Continuous learning from deployed sensors

**Capabilities:**
1. **Smell-to-Language:** "This wine has notes of blackberry, oak, and vanilla"
2. **Language-to-Molecular:** Design fragrance matching "fresh ocean breeze at sunset"
3. **Smell-to-Smell:** Find similar scents, predict combinations
4. **Anomaly Detection:** Identify unknown/dangerous compounds

### Hardware Innovation: ScentSense

**Sensor Array:**
- 256 chemo-resistive sensors
- Each sensor functionalized with different receptor molecules
- Combined pattern creates unique "smell fingerprint"
- Machine learning decodes fingerprint to compounds

**Key Specs:**
- Detection limit: 1 ppb (parts per billion)
- Response time: 50ms
- Compounds detected: 10,000+
- Power consumption: 100mW
- Form factor: 10mm × 10mm × 3mm

**Manufacturing:**
- MEMS fabrication (TSMC partnership)
- Unit cost at scale: $5-10
- 1M+ units/month capacity by Year 2
