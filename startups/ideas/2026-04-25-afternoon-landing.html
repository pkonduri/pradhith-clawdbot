The Problem

Repair Is Fundamentally Broken

78% of consumers want to repair rather than replace. But the infrastructure doesn't exist to help them.

🔍

No Diagnosis

Is it the battery? The screen? The motherboard? Without expert knowledge, consumers are flying blind. Average troubleshooting takes 15+ minutes with no guarantee.

🔒

Locked Out

Proprietary screws, glued components, serialized parts. OEMs design devices to be unrepairable, turning $20 fixes into $500 replacements.

📚

No Guidance

YouTube videos are hit-or-miss, often outdated. Official documentation is hoarded. One wrong step can brick a device permanently.

🎰

Parts Roulette

Thousands of SKUs, quality varies wildly, counterfeit parts abound. Finding the right part at a fair price feels like gambling.

🏪

Fragmented Shops

Independent repair shops operate without modern tools. No AI diagnosis, no parts network, no training infrastructure.

⚖️

Compliance Chaos

Right-to-repair laws passed in 30+ states. OEMs scrambling to comply with no infrastructure to support it.
