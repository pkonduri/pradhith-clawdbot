## The Problem: AI Colonialism

### The Dependency Crisis

The world's 195 countries are rapidly dividing into two categories:
- **AI Superpowers** (US, China) who control the technology
- **AI Dependents** (everyone else) who rent it

This creates existential risks:

**National Security Vulnerabilities**
- Defense systems running on foreign AI can be compromised
- Critical infrastructure depends on foreign model providers
- Intelligence operations are visible to foreign companies
- Supply chain disruptions can cripple entire nations

**Economic Extraction**
- $500B+ annually flows to US/Chinese AI providers
- Local AI talent emigrates to where the models are built
- Innovation happens elsewhere, nations just consume
- No capture of AI-driven productivity gains

**Cultural Erosion**
- Models trained on English/Mandarin data
- Local languages and contexts poorly served
- Cultural values embedded by foreign developers
- Loss of informational sovereignty

### The Failed DIY Approach

Countries that try to build AI independently face:

| Challenge | Reality |
|-----------|---------|
| Compute | $10B+ to build competitive training clusters |
| Talent | Top researchers command $5M+ packages |
| Data | Years to collect and curate quality datasets |
| Time | 3-5 years to reach frontier capabilities |
| Iteration | No institutional knowledge, constant restarts |

**Result:** Billions wasted on fragmented initiatives that never reach production.
