## Technology Deep Dive

### The Universal Vaccine Challenge

Traditional vaccines target specific pathogen strains. Universal vaccines must identify "conserved epitopes" — protein regions that remain stable across all variants and related species.

**Why This Is Hard:**
- Conserved regions are often poorly immunogenic (the immune system ignores them)
- Cross-reactive antibodies can cause harmful immune responses
- Balancing breadth vs. potency requires multi-objective optimization

**How VaccineOS Solves It:**

#### Conserved Epitope Discovery
```python
# Simplified VaccineOS workflow
pathogen_family = "Coronavirus"
target_species = ["SARS-CoV-2", "SARS-CoV-1", "MERS", "HCoV-229E", "bat_coronaviruses"]

# Step 1: Multi-sequence alignment across species
conserved_regions = PathogenAI.find_conserved(target_species, 
                                               conservation_threshold=0.95,
                                               structural_constraints=True)

# Step 2: Predict immunogenicity of conserved regions
immunogenic_epitopes = ImmunoSim.predict_immunogenicity(
    epitopes=conserved_regions,
    hla_coverage=0.99,  # Cover 99% of human HLA types
    t_cell=True,
    b_cell=True
)

# Step 3: Design optimal antigen presenting conserved epitopes
vaccine_candidate = VaccineOS.design_antigen(
    target_epitopes=immunogenic_epitopes,
    platform="mRNA",
    optimize_for=["breadth", "durability", "safety"],
    manufacturing_constraints=True
)
```
