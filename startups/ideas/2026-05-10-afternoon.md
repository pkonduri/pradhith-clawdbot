## 🚨 The Problem

Extreme heat has quietly become the deadliest natural disaster on Earth.

**The Crisis:**
- **1,600+ heat-related deaths annually** in the US alone (CDC estimates 10x underreported)
- **$100B+ in annual productivity losses** from outdoor workers unable to safely work
- **13% of US workers** are exposed to dangerous heat conditions
- **Heat waves increasing 3x** since 1960s; 2025-2026 saw record-breaking global temperatures
- **Air conditioning demand** projected to triple globally by 2050

**Why It's Getting Worse:**
- Urban heat islands amplify temperatures 10-20°F above surrounding areas
- Aging infrastructure (power grids, roads) fails under thermal stress
- Agricultural losses from crop heat stress: $15B+ annually in the US
- Indoor spaces without AC become deadly during multi-day heat events
- Construction, logistics, agriculture, and delivery workers face impossible choices

**The Regulatory Tsunami:**
- OSHA finalizing first-ever federal heat safety standard in 2026
- California, Texas, Florida mandating workplace heat monitoring
- EU Fit for 55 requiring heat adaptation plans
- Insurance companies repricing heat risk dramatically

**Current Solutions Are Broken:**
- Weather forecasts give location-level predictions, not asset-specific guidance
- Worker safety programs are reactive and compliance-focused
- Cities lack integrated heat response systems
- No coordination between energy, health, transport, and emergency services

**The gap:** No one is building the AI operating system that turns heat from a crisis into a manageable, predictable, actionable variable across all stakeholders.
