## The Problem

### The $500B Infrastructure Nobody Thinks About

The cold chain touches everything from the mRNA vaccine in your arm to the strawberries on your table. Yet it operates on infrastructure designed for a different era:

**Fragmentation is Fatal**
- Average pharmaceutical shipment passes through 7+ handoffs
- Each handoff uses different monitoring systems
- No unified visibility = billions in losses

**Reactive, Not Predictive**
- Current systems alert AFTER temperature excursions
- By then, the damage is done
- Perishables can't be "un-spoiled"

**Climate Chaos**
- Extreme weather events increased 400% in 30 years
- Traditional cold chain planning assumes stable conditions
- One heat wave can destroy millions in inventory

**Regulatory Nightmare**
- FDA, EU GDP, WHO PQS all have different requirements
- Compliance is manual, expensive, error-prone
- One documentation failure can halt entire shipments

## Appendix

### Industry Terminology

- **Cold Chain**: Temperature-controlled supply chain
- **Excursion**: Temperature deviation outside acceptable range
- **Reefer**: Refrigerated shipping container
- **GDP**: Good Distribution Practice (EU pharma regulation)
- **FSMA**: Food Safety Modernization Act (US)
- **WHO PQS**: World Health Organization Performance, Quality, Safety

| Region | Regulation | Key Requirements |
|--------|------------|------------------|
