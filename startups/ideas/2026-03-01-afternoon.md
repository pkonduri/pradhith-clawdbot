## The Problem

### Parenting in 2026 is Broken

**1. The Village is Gone**
Previous generations had extended family, neighbors, and community wisdom. Today's parents are isolated, especially remote workers. 65% of new parents report feeling "completely unprepared" for parenthood.

**2. Information Overload Creates Anxiety**
- 10,000+ parenting books in print
- 2.5 million parenting articles published annually
- Contradictory advice on every topic (sleep training alone has 50+ conflicting methodologies)
- Result: 76% of parents report decision paralysis

**3. The Childcare Crisis**
- Average US daycare costs $15,000-$25,000/year
- 51% of Americans live in "childcare deserts"
- Parents spend 10+ hours/week on childcare logistics alone

**4. Developmental Gaps Go Unnoticed**
- Early intervention is critical — 80% of brain development happens by age 3
- Yet most developmental delays aren't caught until kindergarten
- 1 in 6 children have a developmental disability
- Parents miss 40% of milestone delays without proper tracking

**5. Parental Burnout is an Epidemic**
- 66% of working parents report burnout (up from 31% in 2019)
- "Mental load" falls disproportionately on mothers
- Parenting is the #1 cause of marital stress
