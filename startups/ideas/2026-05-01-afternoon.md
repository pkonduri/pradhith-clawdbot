## The $2 Trillion Time Bomb

Every day, $3 trillion in transactions flow through systems written in COBOL. Banks, insurers, governments, and Fortune 500 companies run on code older than their youngest employees. The problem? The developers who built these systems are retiring at 10,000 per day, and no one is learning COBOL anymore.

**The crisis is now:**
- 95% of ATM transactions touch COBOL code
- 80% of in-person transactions use COBOL
- The average mainframe developer is 58 years old
- Estimated $800B in technical debt locked in legacy systems
- 75% of Fortune 500 modernization projects fail

**The old approach is broken:**
- Manual code archaeology takes years
- "Lift and shift" creates cloud-hosted legacy nightmares
- Complete rewrites cost hundreds of millions and often fail
- Consulting firms charge $500/hr and still can't deliver

**LegacyLift AI is the answer:** An autonomous platform that understands legacy systems at the architectural, business logic, and code levelвҖ”then transforms them into modern applications with zero business disruption.
