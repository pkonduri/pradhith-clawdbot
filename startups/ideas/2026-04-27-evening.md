Every major decision—corporate strategy, investment allocation, supply chain planning, policy design—involves forecasting the future. Today, these forecasts rely on expert opinion, historical models, and gut instinct. The results are often wrong, and the failures are expensive.

## Technology Deep Dive

### Data Infrastructure

**Challenge:** Prediction markets use different APIs, contract structures, and settlement mechanisms. Normalizing this data is non-trivial.

**Our Approach:**

1. **Universal Event Ontology**
   - Proprietary knowledge graph mapping events across platforms
   - NLP-based contract parsing to identify equivalent markets
   - Human-in-the-loop verification for high-stakes mappings

2. **Real-time Ingestion**
   - WebSocket connections to all major platforms
   - Sub-100ms latency for price updates
   - Redundant ingestion paths for reliability

3. **Historical Archive**
   - Complete trade history for all covered markets
   - Reconstructed order books where available
   - Event resolution database with ground truth outcomes

### Intelligence Engine

**Forecast Aggregation:**
- Bayesian model combining multiple market signals
- Weights calibrated on historical accuracy
- Adjustments for liquidity, market age, and known biases

**Arbitrage Detection:**
- Real-time cross-platform price comparison
- Execution cost modeling (fees, slippage, withdrawal times)
- Risk-adjusted opportunity scoring

**Signal Extraction:**
- Sharp money detection (identifying informed traders)
- Sentiment analysis from social/news
- Correlation analysis with related markets

### Agent Orchestration

**Execution Layer:**
- Unified order interface across all platforms
- Smart order routing for best execution
- Position management and reconciliation

**Agent Runtime:**
- Sandboxed execution environment
- Resource limits and rate limiting
- Real-time monitoring and kill switches

## Regulatory Strategy

### Current Landscape

Prediction markets exist in regulatory gray zones:
- **Kalshi:** CFTC-regulated DCM (designated contract market)
- **Polymarket:** Offshore, US users technically prohibited
- **PredictIt:** Operating under no-action letter (expiring)
- **Sports Betting:** State-by-state legalization

### Our Approach

**1. Platform-Agnostic:**
- We don't operate markets—we aggregate data and facilitate execution
- Similar to how Bloomberg doesn't operate exchanges

**2. Compliance Infrastructure:**
- KYC/AML integration for execution features
- Geo-restriction for unlicensed markets
- Audit trails and reporting

**3. Regulatory Engagement:**
- Proactive dialogue with CFTC and state regulators
- Supporting industry standards development
- Legal structure designed for evolving requirements

**4. Contingency Planning:**
- Architecture allows rapid market inclusion/exclusion
- Revenue diversification reduces single-platform dependence
- Enterprise API functions regardless of retail market status
