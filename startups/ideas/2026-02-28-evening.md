## 🔥 The Problem: AI's Thermodynamic Crisis

The AI revolution is hitting a wall — and it's not compute, it's **cooling**.

### The Scale of the Crisis

- **AI Training Clusters**: A single GPT-5-class training run generates enough heat to warm 10,000 homes
- **Cooling = 40% of TCO**: Datacenters spend $50B+ annually just keeping servers from melting
- **Water Consumption**: A single hyperscale datacenter uses 5M gallons of water daily — during global water scarcity
- **PUE Stagnation**: Despite decades of effort, average datacenter PUE is stuck at 1.58 (ideal is 1.0)
- **Stranded Capacity**: 30% of planned AI datacenter capacity is delayed due to cooling constraints

### Why Now?

1. **AI Compute Explosion**: Global AI datacenter capacity growing 35% CAGR through 2030
2. **Nvidia Blackwell/Rubin**: Next-gen chips need 1,200W+ per GPU — 3x previous generation
3. **Water Restrictions**: Major markets (Arizona, Texas, Europe) implementing datacenter water bans
4. **Energy Costs**: Cooling electricity costs doubled since 2024
5. **Climate Pressure**: Hyperscalers racing to carbon-neutral commitments

### The Cooling Complexity Problem

Modern datacenter cooling isn't simple HVAC. It's a chaotic system with:

- **Thousands of variables**: Ambient temp, humidity, workload, airflow, equipment age, weather forecasts
- **Multi-physics interactions**: Thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, electrical systems
- **Conflicting objectives**: Minimize energy, minimize water, maximize reliability, meet SLAs
- **Dynamic loads**: AI workloads create unpredictable heat patterns
- **Legacy infrastructure**: Most facilities mix old and new equipment

**Human operators can't optimize this. Neither can rule-based systems. It requires AI.**
