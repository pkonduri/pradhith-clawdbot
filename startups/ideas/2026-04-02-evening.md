## The Problem

### The Invisible Demographic Crisis

By 2030, **1 in 5 Americans over 65 will be aging without children or a spouse**. This includes:
- Never-married/never-partnered individuals
- LGBTQ+ elders estranged from biological family
- Parents who outlived their children
- Those with distant or unavailable family
- The voluntarily childfree generation now reaching retirement

**The Traditional Care Model Is Broken for Solo Agers:**

| Traditional Assumption | Solo Ager Reality |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Family coordinates care | No default coordinator exists |
| Children manage finances | No one has visibility into accounts |
| Spouse notices health changes | No one sees the early warning signs |
| Family responds to emergencies | 911 is the only option |
| Adult children advocate with doctors | No one questions medical decisions |
| Estate handled by next-of-kin | Complex legal arrangements required |

### The Current Solutions Are Inadequate

**Medical alert devices:** Reactive, not proactive. Only useful after a fall.  
**Senior living communities:** Many solo agers want to age in place, not institutionalize.  
**Care management apps:** Assume family caregiver users. Solo agers ARE the user.  
**Financial advisors:** Transactional relationships, not holistic life management.  
**Senior services:** Fragmented, bureaucratic, hard to navigate solo.

### The Stakes Are High

- **50% of solo agers** report feeling socially isolated
- **3x higher risk** of cognitive decline from loneliness
- **2x more likely** to be victims of financial exploitation
- **Average emergency response time** is 10+ hours when no one checks in
- **$50,000+** in unnecessary end-of-life costs from lack of planning
