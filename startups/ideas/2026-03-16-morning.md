## The Problem

### The Great Infrastructure Race

The AI industry is facing its most existential constraint: **physical infrastructure cannot keep pace with compute demand**.

**The Bottlenecks:**

1. **Power Scarcity** — AI datacenters need 100MW-1GW+ of reliable power. Grid capacity in most regions is exhausted. New power takes 5-10 years to build.

2. **Land Complexity** — Suitable sites need the right zoning, environmental clearances, water access, fiber connectivity, and proximity to transmission infrastructure.

3. **Regulatory Maze** — Permitting involves dozens of federal, state, and local agencies. A single missed requirement can delay projects by years.

4. **Climate Risk** — Sites must be evaluated for long-term viability: flooding, extreme heat, water stress, wildfire exposure.

5. **Competition** — Every major tech company is hunting the same sites. First-mover advantage determines who gets the best locations.

### Current Process is Broken

Today, finding and securing a datacenter site involves:

- **6-18 months** of site identification and evaluation
- **$2-5M** in consulting fees, studies, and preliminary work
- **Dozens of specialists**: land brokers, power consultants, environmental engineers, attorneys, lobbyists
- **High failure rate**: 60%+ of evaluated sites don't work out
- **Information asymmetry**: Hyperscalers have armies of scouts; everyone else is flying blind

**The cost of delay is catastrophic.** Every month without additional compute capacity is billions in lost AI revenue and competitive position.
